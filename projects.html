Service Innovation for Sheetzclose

Convenience stores are a way of life. The services that they offer busy people have expanded to include payless, customized, and delivery options. However, not much is known about how different data streams, from loyalty cards, credit cards, and operations, might be used to better personalize the services offered.

To address this problem, we researched and collaboratively designed new data-driven services with Sheetz, a growing convenience store chain in central PA. Sheetz was the first to innovate personalized orders with its kiosk system around 2000.

Our solutions explored services for a variety of stakeholders with the goal of innovating around Sheetz’s business model. I worked in teams to understand the current social, economic, and technological factors surrounding convenience store shopping. I received feedback and support from our clients at Sheetz.

My goal in this project was to envision new services, apps, and platforms that leverage Sheetz’s expertise and unique position in the convenience store space, and potentially, data from separate sensors and/or service ecosystems into your new service or platform. My service description defined how data is used to increase or activate value within my service.
